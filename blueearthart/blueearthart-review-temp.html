Josh Overington
Eureka, CA, US

Joshua Overington is a scientific illustrator and interpretive designer based out of California. He uses his art to foster a love for the natural world and its many beautiful intricacies. He is, as we all are, clearly confused, and finds solace in creating beauty and bringing joy through art.

Joshua received his undergraduate degree from Humboldt State University (HSU) in environmental education and interpretation with a minor in studio art. He is currently participating in the Scientific Illustration program at California State University Monterey Bay (CSUMB).

While attending HSU he worked as a research assistant for the Native American Studies department. He designed material for the North Coast Otter Project junior ranger booklet and the Humboldt State Waste Reduction and Resource Awareness Program (WRRAP). He also designed interpretive signage for the Humboldt Botanical Gardens and illustrated an interpretive storybook with the Redwood Community Action Agency.

program spotlight

'program spotlight' highlights programs supporting visual artists who critically engage with marine ecosystems

California Seaweed Festival

The California Seaweed Festival is a free,  family-friendly event celebrating the seaweed diversity of California! The festival showcases seaweed ecology as well as applications in art, cooking, farming, science, sustainable technology, and conservation.

Our festival team wants to share the beauty and diversity of seaweeds on our coasts, how seaweeds enrich coastal environments making seawater cleaner and a better habitat for hundreds of other species. We imagine a California where seaweed is a kitchen staple, a conversation-starter, a career field, and a conservation focus. The California Seaweed Festival engages families, learners, entrepreneurs, activists, and more in meaningful ways that enact stewardship while electrifying a nascent seaweed industry. We work to bolster the Blue Economy and forge a California populace that is primed for sustainable seaweed use and appreciation.
