Connecting ocean art to ocean tech

Local artist Sian Storey joined Blue Robotics and Beautify Earth at the bi-annual Ocean Business conference in Southampton, UK to paint one of two murals connecting ocean art to technology. This piece is part of a series of work titled 'Ionian Bay.' This mural is part of the Blue Earth Street Art program that supports mural and street artists critically exploring marine ecosystems and technology.

Interested in getting involved in future Blue Earth Street Art projects? If you're an artist, sponsor, or building owner with an interest in marine ecosystems and technology get in touch at hello@beautifyearth.com.

artist of the week

Sian Storey
Basingstoke, England, UK

Blending the beauty of the natural world with ethereal colour, Sian Storey is a Hampshire based artist who captures creatures as you’ve never seen them before. Festooned with beautiful flora, her bird and animal murals are bursting with life. Sian’s work has featured in national UK media including The Guardian, The Times and BBC.

READ -- Critical Thinking

by Lamonica Curator, Substack -- "Critical thinking involves breaking things down into possible approaches, testing them and finding our coherent answers. This journey—even when it leads to uncomfortable or unexpected conclusions—is where real value lies. So why not practice on art? By teaching ourselves to look at art critically, we cultivate the skills to look at life critically." Read the full article at the link above.

by Ariel Parrella-Aureli, Block Club Chicago -- Friends and family of Dont Fret are remembering him as a loving, humorous and helping hand who enjoyed soccer, fishing and sausages — all reflected in his work.

READ -- Instagram changed contemporary painting

by Marina Roca Die, Color Matters -- Immediacy, content v. art, size, likes, ads, and dialogue are factors explored in this article on the debated conversation around digital art consumption and communications.

BOOK CLUB - The Dispossessed

by Ursula K. Le Guin -- The Dispossessed explores the tension between individual freedom and social responsibility, examining the impact of ideology on personal and collective life. Through themes of isolation, idealism, and the struggle for liberation, it also touches on the role of art as a form of expression and a means of challenging societal constraints, questioning whether true creativity can exist within rigid systems.

support local art programs

Beautify Earth was built by and for our community with a shared vision of supporting local artists and art initiatives. Our team is committed to not only creating more art in communties but advancing practices of how and why we produce art in public space. Every donation makes a difference and allows us to support more artists and engage more communities. To discuss supporting at a larger scale or partnerships please email hello@beautifyearth.com.

  • BEverse: a digital network facilitating the creation and exploration of local art, artists, walls, and resources
  • Beautify schools: creating murals in schools with local artists to promote arts education and self-expression
  • Blue Earth Street Art: connecting communities to underwater ecosystems through local mural and street art initiatives around the world
  • Santa Monica Street Art: promoting opportunities for artists and the community to engage with and develop street art that reflects the history, spirit, and people of Santa Monica
