Welcome to your weekly scooop of mural & street art, opps, events, and media curated by the BE team. Since 2012, Beautify Earth has supported art on the streets through local art productions, networks, and media. Edited by Moral Masuoka. Header art by Mary Iverson.

sound scooop

On repeat this week is the 1972 ablbum 'Introspection' by Luiz Bonfá (1922–2001). Bonfá was a Brazilian guitarist and composer renowned for his role in the development of bossa nova. His  guitar style blended classical training with Brazilian rhythms, creating a distinctive, emotive sound.

artist of the week

Mary Iverson
Seattle, WA, US

My paintings explore the balance between the environment and industrial activities, inspiring conversations about our complicated relationship with nature. I enjoy designing murals that bring nature and color into industrial spaces. My main sources of inspiration are the three magnificent National Parks close to home: Rainier, Olympic, and North Cascades National Parks, and the colorful geometry of the Port of Seattle.

media finds to feed your mind

READ -- Graffiti Ethos: The Art of Disobedience.

by Giulia Blocal, Beyond the Walls -- "This month’s editorial was sparked by the graffiti film “The Art of Disobedience”, created by and about Rome’s graffiti king, GECO—a documentary currently touring cinemas across Italy and Europe. The film serves as a starting point for a reflection on the ethos of graffiti, what has always fascinated me about this world, and what we can learn from it." Read more

READ -- How This City’s Private Sector Is Rewriting The Rules Of Public Art

by Byron Armstrong, Forbes -- "In a city celebrated for its cultural diversity, Toronto’s most compelling art experiences are increasingly unfolding far beyond traditional museums and galleries. They pulse through the rush of commuters at Union Station, resonate in the bustling corridors of suburban shopping malls, and transform sterile office plazas into destinations. This shift isn’t accidental – it’s driven by a pioneering coalition of private sector giants and grassroots arts organizers who are proving that commerce and culture, far from being at odds, can create vibrant, accessible, and deeply meaningful artistic encounters for millions." support local art programs

Beautify Earth was built by and for our community with a shared vision of supporting local artists and art initiatives. Our team is committed to not only creating more art in communties but advancing practices of how and why we produce art in public space. Every donation makes a difference and allows us to support more artists and engage more communities. To discuss supporting at a larger scale or partnerships please email hello@beautifyearth.com.

  • BEverse: a digital network facilitating the creation and exploration of local art, artists, walls, and resources
  • Beautify schools: creating murals in schools with local artists to promote arts education and self-expression
  • Blue Earth Arts: connecting communities to underwater ecosystems through local mural and street art initiatives around the world
  • Santa Monica Mural Arts: promoting opportunities for artists and the community to engage with and develop street art that reflects the history, spirit, and people of Santa Monica
