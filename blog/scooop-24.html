sound scooop

"'What was old is new again'… The old adage is so tired that nobody takes its proper measure: all people hear is the word “old”, when the important one is “new”. Making new things out of old things is an act of alchemy. You take rusted lead and transform it into sparkling gold… The Velvet Underground, The Stooges, The Standells and other garage heroes; Ennio Morricone, John Barry and 1960’s Italian songs; The Cramps, of course, Nick Cave, Gainsbourg and tonic psych rock: Lionel and Marie from the Limiñanas know them all by heart, they worship them. They drink from that well and, after a good kneading and pounding, they make something else out of it."

feast your eyes on this

World-renowned mural artist Julia Volchkova completed a new monumental mural in Sabak Bernam, continuing her series of works dedicated to Malaysian culture. The new mural, located on one of the city’s central walls, is a breathtaking artwork filled with deep symbolism and emotional intensity. It is a composite image from a series of murals depicting fishermen, which Julia discovered while traveling and exploring the life and culture of Malaysia’s people. The mural took over a month of continuous work, in a tropical climate during the rainy season, which made the work more difficult and making it the longest project in the artist’s career.“I draw inspiration from people, their stories, and the traditions that shape the unique identity of each place. This mural is a tribute to Malaysian culture and its rich heritage,” says Julia Volchkova.

artist of the week

Kristine Campbell
Los Angeles, CA, US

"Hi! I’m Kristine, an LA-based, self-taught multidisciplinary artist originally from Chicago. My work blends whimsical realism, symbolic narratives, and creative portraiture, thoughtfully layered with rich composition and color. I’ve had the opportunity to create murals and fine art for commercial, public, and high-end residential spaces, collaborating with clients like Westfield, Sony Pictures, AON, and PETA. Driven by a passion for storytelling, my work reflects the beauty and complexity of nature and the human experience and its connection to the world around us. I love creating pieces that ignite conversation, emotion, and transform spaces. I’m always excited to collaborate on new projects and am available to travel as needed—let’s create!"

support local art programs

Beautify Earth was built by and for our community with a shared vision of supporting local artists and art initiatives. Our team is committed to not only creating more art in communties but advancing practices of how and why we produce art in public space. Every donation makes a difference and allows us to support more artists and engage more communities. To discuss supporting at a larger scale or partnerships please email hello@beautifyearth.com.

  • BEverse: a digital network facilitating the creation and exploration of local art, artists, walls, and resources
  • Beautify schools: creating murals in schools with local artists to promote arts education and self-expression
  • Blue Earth Street Art: connecting communities to underwater ecosystems through local mural and street art initiatives around the world
  • Santa Monica Street Art: promoting opportunities for artists and the community to engage with and develop street art that reflects the history, spirit, and people of Santa Monica
