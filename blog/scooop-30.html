artist of the week

Miles Lewis
Los Angeles, CA, US

Miles is an artist and an art and environmental educator. He grew up in LA and is dedicated to beautifying and improving the city. He has a broad technical and imaginative range and loves a challenge. His work is based in line drawing and ranges between simple color accents to full, strong color. His four great passions are art, education, critical/creative thinking, and environmental stewardship.

Open House with SPARC at Bergamot Station

May 31, 2025
Santa Monica, CA, US

"Join us for a behind-the-scenes look at the artistic process behind the expansion of the Great Wall of Los Angeles mural as we transition from rendering the energy of the 1960s to the inspiring stories, people, and events of the 1970s. Meet our team, tour the studio, and learn more about the stories and histories being brought to life through this monumental mural project.  "

CVTA Festival

May 30 - June 1, 2025
Civitacampomarano, Italy

"A street art festival can only be born through the participation and sharing of the project by an entire community. In this perspective, the inhabitants of Civitacampomarano themselves competed to make the most beautiful wall, the most enchanting glimpse, the most precious panorama available to the artists. Each of the six artists is invited to perform their own intervention on the skin of the ancient village over the four days in which the event will take place, working in close contact with the locals."

BOSTON PUBLIC ART TRIENNIAL

May 22 - October 31, 2025
Boston, MA, US

"From May to October 2025, the inaugural Boston Public Art Triennial will present 15 compelling commissions sprawled across the city. The theme of The Triennial 2025 is The Exchange, an artist-driven and expert-supported exhibition of extraordinary cross-disciplinary public art projects that break down social and professional barriers to promote collaboration and strengthen community resilience."

support local art programs

Beautify Earth was built by and for our community with a shared vision of supporting local artists and art initiatives. Our team is committed to not only creating more art in communties but advancing practices of how and why we produce art in public space. Every donation makes a difference and allows us to support more artists and engage more communities. To discuss supporting at a larger scale or partnerships please email hello@beautifyearth.com.

  • BEverse: a digital network facilitating the creation and exploration of local art, artists, walls, and resources
  • Beautify schools: creating murals in schools with local artists to promote arts education and self-expression
  • Blue Earth Street Art: connecting communities to underwater ecosystems through local mural and street art initiatives around the world
  • Santa Monica Street Art: promoting opportunities for artists and the community to engage with and develop street art that reflects the history, spirit, and people of Santa Monica
